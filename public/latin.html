Although discouraged, an older system of Latin (or other origin) names for some cations is still commonly seen. For many transition and post-transition monoatomic cations with at least two common oxidation states the following pattern is observed.

The ion name begins with the root of the element name (many of these use the Latin root of the element name), followed by the suffix "-ous" for the lower of the two common oxidation states, or the suffix "-ic" for the higher of the two common oxidation states.

Following is a list of those examples I could find-it may not be exhaustive. I have included the Latin element name where applicable.

Of additional importance, be aware that the official names of some polyatomic oxoanions and complex ions do include the latinized form of the name of the central element.
